General notary questions

A notary public is a state-commissioned official authorized to verify the identity of signers, witness signatures, and apply an official seal to documents. Notarization confirms that the person signing is who they say they are and that they signed willingly. It does not mean the notary has reviewed the document for legal accuracy.
A valid, current, government-issued photo ID. Make sure your document is unsigned before the appointment. Most notarizations require you to sign in front of the notary, so signing in advance may invalidate the notarization.

What to Bring to Any Notary Appointment

  • A valid, current, government-issued photo ID (driver's license, state ID, or passport)
  • Your document, unsigned
  • Any co-signers who need to be present, each with their own valid ID
  • Payment if required for your appointment type

How to Prepare for a Loan Signing

  • Have your government-issued photo ID ready
  • Do not sign any documents before the signing agent arrives
  • Set aside 45 to 90 minutes depending on the loan type
  • Review your closing disclosure in advance so the numbers are not a surprise
  • If something looks wrong in the documents, say so before signing

What a Notary Can and Cannot Do

A notary can verify your identity, witness your signature, and apply an official seal to your document. A notary cannot give legal advice, review your document for errors, tell you whether you should sign, or draft legal documents. If you have questions about the content of a document, consult an attorney before your appointment.

How to Prepare for a RON Appointment

  • Test your camera and microphone before the appointment time
  • Have your government-issued photo ID within reach to hold up to the camera
  • Be in a quiet, well-lit location
  • Have your document ready but unsigned
  • Check your email for the session link sent with your booking confirmation

Signing at a Hospital or Care Facility: What to Know

Mobile notary appointments at hospitals, nursing homes, and assisted living facilities are available throughout the OKC metro. When booking, note the facility name, address, and room or unit number if known. If the signer requires special accommodations or has limited capacity to communicate, let us know in advance so the appointment can be handled appropriately. A valid ID is still required.
